Why does this exist?

On 1 April 2026, India's ban on non-certified CCTV cameras came into full effect. This is the result of three government directives:

Gazette Notification
7 March & 9 April 2024

Amended the Public Procurement Order for CCTVs (7 March) and added CCTV cameras to the Compulsory Registration Order (CRO), 2021 (9 April), defining Essential Requirements for security including mandatory SoC origin disclosure.

MeitY Office Memorandum
4 February 2026

Withdrew all transition relaxations (No. W-18/26/2025-IPHW), setting 1 April 2026 as the final deadline. No internet-connected CCTV camera can be sold in India without STQC certification.

PIB Press Release
25 March 2026

Confirmed that all CCTV devices must undergo testing at accredited laboratories and disclose the origin of critical components like the SoC.

Under the new framework, all CCTV devices must meet the Essential Requirements for security, including disclosure of SoC origin and testing at STQC-accredited laboratories. Devices that do not meet these certification requirements cannot be legally sold or installed in India from this date. This map documents how many non-certified, internet-facing surveillance devices remain accessible as of the enforcement deadline.

The directive applies to new sales and installations. Existing cameras already deployed are not subject to seizure, though they may not receive future firmware or security updates from their manufacturers.
